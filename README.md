### Communication
All members will remain reachable via the team’s mobile group chat and will check messages daily. Members are expected to be generally attentive to text messages, unless the individual is facing serious consequences from unavoidable emergencies. Key decisions, task assignments, and deadlines must be summarized in writing in the group chat and must be shared in the Google Drive Folder. It is understandable that other classes may interfere with the group project; however, it also must be understood that this project is an important part of our grade. Communication is key to a cohesive group. If good communication is made with group members about absences, exceptions can be made.  It is also important that we delegate the scheduling of deadlines and are all on the same page about when work is due.

### Deadline
It is understandable that other classes may interfere with the group project; however, it also must be understood that this project is an important part of our grade.  If anybody is having a hard time balancing their workload, cannot meet a deadline, or a block in their schedule arises, they must notify the team at least 24 hours in advance to allow reassignment or scope adjustment. Most importantly, if any group member does not do their part, understand that the weight of this work falls on the rest of the group. Failure to communicate or complete assigned work without any notice may result in serious consequences and may be reported. A genuine effort should be noticeable to the other members in the group. All team members are expected to contribute to the overall efforts of the group. We understand that everyone may come from different backgrounds and have different skill sets, so it is important that any team member requests help if needed. Drafts for major deliverables should be uploaded at least 12 hours before the final submission time to allow peer review. Team members are also expected to review each other’s work for clarity, correctness, and consistency.
